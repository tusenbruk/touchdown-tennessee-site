## They Weren't Supposed to Be There

The 1999 Titans had no business being in a Super Bowl conversation in January. They finished 8-8 the year before. Steve McNair missed half of 1998 with injuries. The city of Nashville still called them the Oilers in their heads.

Then Eddie George ran for 1,304 yards. McNair stopped getting hurt long enough to become a problem. And Jeff Fisher coached like a man who knew exactly what he had.

Thirteen wins. Home field. A defense that suffocated people.

## The Miracle Nobody Remembers Correctly

Everybody remembers the Lateral. January 8, 2000. Wild card game in Nashville. Buffalo kicks off. Lorenzo Neal fields it. Hands it to Frank Wycheck. Wycheck throws it across the field to Kevin Dyson. Dyson runs 75 yards for the score.

Titans win 22-16.

Here's what people forget: that play shouldn't have had to happen. The Titans trailed because they played sloppy for three quarters against a team they should have beaten cleanly. The miracle bailed them out of their own mediocrity.

That's the real story. This team was good enough to win ugly and lucky enough to survive it.

## What the Run Actually Required

After Buffalo, they beat Indianapolis. Then Jacksonville in the AFC Championship — a cold, physical game that McNair owned with his legs as much as his arm.

George carried the ball 27 times in that game. Twenty-seven. Nobody does that anymore. He ran through people who outweighed him. He ran until there was nothing left to argue about.

The Titans won 33-14. They weren't sneaking into the Super Bowl. They beat the door down.

## The One Yard

Super Bowl XXXIV. The Rams were faster and more explosive. Kurt Warner was in the middle of something historic. Most people picked St. Louis.

The Titans were down 23-16 with seconds left. McNair scrambled, dumped it to Dyson at the five. Dyson caught it, turned, ran.

Mike Jones wrapped him up at the one.

Game over.

One yard. That's the distance between a dynasty and a footnote.

## What It Actually Means

That team never got back to a Super Bowl. McNair got beaten up until his body quit. George slowed down. The window closed the way windows do — quietly, without announcement.

But here's the truth that still sits with me: that 1999 squad was the most complete Titans team this franchise has ever put together. Built on toughness, not talent. Constructed for winning, not marketing.

They came one yard short.

The question worth asking is whether this organization has ever been willing to build that way again.
