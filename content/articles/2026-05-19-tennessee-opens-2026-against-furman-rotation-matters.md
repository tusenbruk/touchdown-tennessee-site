Tennessee kicks off the 2026 season at home against Furman on September 5th. Standard FCS opener, standard result expected. But this game matters for reasons beyond the final score.

## What You're Actually Watching For

Furman runs a physical, disciplined program out of the Southern Conference. They won't beat Tennessee, but they'll give the staff a live look at depth chart questions that spring ball couldn't answer.

Watch the second-team offensive line. Tennessee will need 8-9 guys ready for SEC play, and Furman's defensive front — always well-coached under Clay Hendrix — provides legitimate reps without the chaos of a Power Four opponent.

The linebacker rotation deserves attention too. Josh Heupel's defense thrived last year when it could mix personnel packages without drop-off. Furman will run enough option concepts and play-action to test assignment discipline. You'll learn more about the linebacker room in the second quarter than you did all spring.

## The Schedule Context

Tennessee State heads to Georgia the same day. That's the other in-state FCS program, and the contrast tells you everything about how SEC programs view September.

Georgia gets a completely overmatched opponent to work through a script. Tennessee gets a program that's made FCS playoff runs and won't fold after the first punch. The difference might show up in Week 3 or 4 when depth becomes real.

## What Actually Matters

Tickets start at $51 for Neyland — low number for an opener, even against FCS competition. The fanbase knows this game means nothing until it suddenly means everything because a starting guard went down in the third quarter and his backup logged zero meaningful snaps.

Heupel's best teams rotate early and often. This game exists to make sure 70-75 guys are ready when Alabama or Florida comes calling.

The score will be lopsided. The value comes from what happens when the twos and threes check in.
