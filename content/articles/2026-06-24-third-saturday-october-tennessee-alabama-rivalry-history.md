## The Game That Made Alabama What It Is

Bear Bryant needed Tennessee to become Bear Bryant.

People forget that. They remember the championships, the houndstooth hat, the mythology. But in the 1960s, when Bryant was assembling the Alabama program into something permanent, Tennessee was the measuring stick. Not Auburn. Not LSU. Tennessee.

Winning the Third Saturday in October meant you were real. Losing it meant you had work to do.

Bryant went 15-6 against the Vols. He respected the rivalry enough to fear it. That fear was a compliment Tennessee no longer receives.

## What Tennessee Built — And Then Abandoned

From 1926 through 1970, this series was genuinely even. Tennessee won 22 times. Alabama won 23. One tie. The kind of record that makes a rivalry legitimate.

General Neyland defined defensive football in this league while building Tennessee into a national program. His teams didn't just compete with Alabama — they set the standard Alabama eventually chased.

The 1939 Volunteers went undefeated. The 1956 team smashed Alabama 24-0. Johnny Majors, Reggie White, Condredge Holloway — Tennessee produced players who redefined positions.

And then something shifted.

## The Slow Surrender Nobody Named

After the 1990 Tennessee run — Peyton Manning, the 1998 national title, six consecutive wins over Alabama from 1995 to 2001 — the program made a quiet decision without knowing it. It decided facilities and recruiting rankings were enough. That the rivalry would maintain itself.

Alabama under Nick Saban operated like a corporation that identified a hostile takeover target and executed systematically.

Saban went 15-1 against Tennessee. Fifteen. And one of those losses came in 2022, the first time Tennessee won since 2006.

Sixteen years. A generation of Vol fans grew up believing losing to Alabama was simply the natural order.

## The Psychological Weight Nobody Talks About

Here is the uncomfortable part.

Tennessee didn't just lose football games. It lost the belief that it *should* win. There's a difference between a team that loses and a program that accepts losing. The Vols crossed that line somewhere around 2010 and spent a decade on the wrong side of it.

Josh Heupel's 2022 victory mattered not because it snapped a streak, but because it reminded Tennessee fans what it felt like to *expect* to compete.

Bryant needed Tennessee to become great. The question now is whether Tennessee needs Alabama the same way — as the rival that forces you to rebuild yourself from the foundation.

The Third Saturday used to answer that question every October.

It's time it started again.
