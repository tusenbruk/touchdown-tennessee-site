## What the Board Actually Tells You

Forget the grades ESPN handed out in April. Draft grades are fiction. You want the truth, wait five years.

The Titans' drafting history — from the Houston holdovers through the Fisher era through the Jon Robinson rebuild — tells one consistent story. This organization drafts well when it drafts boring. It bleeds when it swings for the highlight reel.

## The Trenches Don't Lie

Keith Bulluck. Kyle Vanden Bosch. Michael Roos. Kevin Mawae came via free agency, but the type is the same — tough, unflashy, productive for a decade.

When the Titans trusted the tape on workers, they won. Roos was a second-round pick in 2005. Nobody threw a parade. He started 140 games. That's the formula.

The 2016 draft — Taylor Lewan, Derrick Henry, Jonnu Smith — gets undersold. Three contributors, two of them cornerstones. That was Robinson at his best. Quiet work. Right players.

## When the Upside Addiction Kicks In

Here's the pattern nobody talks about enough. The Titans don't just miss on busts. They miss on a *specific kind* of bust.

Athletes they believed they could fix.

Vince Young had the Heisman buzz and the Rose Bowl mythology. The front office convinced itself the tools were there. They weren't wrong about the tools. They were wrong about the rest of it. Same song with Jake Locker — arm talent, character, injury history that should've scared someone off. It didn't.

This organization has a weakness for quarterbacks with exceptional physical gifts and unresolved questions. It is a weakness that has cost them a decade of stability at the most important position in sports.

## The Robinson Correction

Jon Robinson understood the disease and tried to cure it. He took Derrick Henry over sexier options. He took Adoree' Jackson when the secondary was bleeding. He didn't always get it right — Corey Davis was a reach at five, full stop — but the philosophy was sound.

Trust the film. Draft the position. Don't draft the promise.

The Ran Carthon era is still being written. But the early returns on Will Levis suggest the upside addiction isn't fully in remission.

## What It Means

Every franchise has a drafting fingerprint. The Titans' fingerprint says: *we build lines and we develop runners and we fall in love with quarterbacks who aren't ready.*

Two out of three isn't enough.

The question isn't whether they can find players. They can. The question is whether they can finally stop talking themselves into ones they can't finish.
