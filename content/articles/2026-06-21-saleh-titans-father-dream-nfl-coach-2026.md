## The Weight He Carries

Robert Saleh didn't just get a head coaching job. He inherited a debt. The kind you owe to a father who believed before anyone else did.

Sam Saleh dreamed of watching his son coach in the NFL. Robert is doing exactly that. Now he's doing it in Nashville, with a franchise that needs a turnaround and a fanbase that's been patient long enough.

That story matters. Don't let anybody tell you it doesn't.

## New City, Real Stakes

The Titans open the 2026 season September 13th at Nissan Stadium against the Jets. Home game. Week 1. Vegas already likes Tennessee — TEN -3, moneyline -170.

That's respect. Modest respect, but respect.

The over/under sits at 39.5. Low number. Tells you something. Books think this is a defensive game, a grind. That fits what Saleh built his reputation on. Defense. Toughness. Identity.

He tried to build that in New York. Didn't get the runway. Nashville is giving him another shot.

## What He's Building Here

Saleh runs a 4-3 base defense. He demands physicality up front. He wants fast linebackers and corners who can press. The Titans have the cap space to build it right this time.

More importantly, he has something the Jets never gave him consistently — stability in the front office and a quarterback situation that isn't a circus.

He can coach. That was never the question in New York. The question was whether the people around him would let him.

## The Father Factor

People underestimate what this kind of motivation does to a coach. Sam Saleh is why Robert kept grinding when New York got ugly. That doesn't evaporate when you change zip codes.

It sharpens you.

Saleh isn't coaching to prove the Jets wrong. He's coaching to make one man proud. That's a cleaner fuel. Burns longer.

## The First Test

Week 1 against the Jets is almost poetic. His old team walks into his new house on the very first Sunday of the season.

The Titans are favored. The crowd will be loud. Saleh will be ready.

But none of that is the real story. The real story is a son doing what his father dreamed. Everything else — the wins, the losses, the spreads — that's just football.

The question now is simple: can Saleh make Tennessee believe the way his father believed in him?
