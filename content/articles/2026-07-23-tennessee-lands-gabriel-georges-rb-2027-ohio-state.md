## The Pick That Stings Columbus

David Gabriel Georges picked Tennessee. Not Ohio State. Not one of the blue-blood programs that spent months recruiting the No. 14 overall prospect in the 2027 class.

Tennessee.

Let that sink in for a second, because this isn't your grandfather's Vols program scraping together a class. This is Josh Heupel going into Ohio State's backyard — figuratively speaking — and ripping the No. 2 running back in the country right out of Ryan Day's hands.

## Why This Matters Beyond the Rankings

Georges isn't just a number on a board. ESPN's evaluators put him at No. 2 among running backs nationally. That's a player programs like Alabama, Georgia, and yes, Ohio State build offenses around.

The fact that Tennessee won this battle tells you something about where this program sits right now. You don't win monthslong recruiting fights against Ohio State by accident. You win them because players believe they can play early, develop fast, and get to the NFL from your program.

Heupel has built that case. The track record with skill players speaks for itself.

## The Ohio State Angle

Here's the uncomfortable question nobody in Columbus wants to answer: what does it mean when you lose a top-15 national prospect to a program that went 15 years without winning anything worth remembering?

It means Tennessee is no longer the easy no. It means Knoxville is a legitimate destination.

Ohio State will recover — they always do. But don't minimize what happened here. This was a prolonged, heated battle. Georges had every opportunity to commit to a program with four national championships in the last six years. He chose the orange.

## What This Does for Tennessee's 2027 Class

Momentum in recruiting is real and it compounds fast. When a prospect ranked inside the top 15 nationally picks your program, the next guy on your board notices. His teammates notice. His high school coach notices.

Tennessee's 2027 class just got a centerpiece. Now the Vols need to build around Georges and show they can sustain this level of recruiting pull into the teeth of SEC competition season after season — not just flash it once.

## The Next Question

Georges is a 2027 prospect, which means the real test is what Heupel does between now and signing day. Can Tennessee keep him committed when Alabama, Georgia, and others come knocking with late pressure?

That's the next fight. But for today, Big Orange Nation earned the right to feel good about this one.
