## Another September Saturday, Another FCS Opponent

Tennessee opens the 2026 season hosting Furman on September 5th. Furman. The FCS Paladins from the Southern Conference.

Meanwhile, Georgia hosts Tennessee State — also FCS — the exact same day in Athens.

You see the pattern here? Two SEC juggernauts. Two guarantee games. Two programs collecting easy wins while the rest of college football figures out who's actually elite.

I've covered SEC football for three decades, and I'll tell you what — this scheduling philosophy is a relic. It's painting by numbers. It's checking a box that says "Win Number One" without asking what it's worth.

## The Soft Open Has Consequences

Look, I understand the business model. Furman gets a paycheck. Tennessee gets a homecoming-style atmosphere in Week One. Neyland fills up with 100,000 fans who want to see the Vols roll. Nobody gets hurt, everybody feels good.

But when Selection Sunday rolls around — or whatever we're calling playoff seating day now — these games matter. Strength of schedule isn't just a tiebreaker anymore. It's the whole conversation.

Georgia can afford Tennessee State on the schedule because they'll play five ranked teams in conference play. Tennessee? We don't know yet. But opening with Furman means you're borrowing credibility from October and November to pay for September's confidence boost.

## The Bigger Question Vol Nation Won't Ask

Here's what bothers me: Tennessee has the resources, the brand, and the stadium to schedule anybody. Furman costs less than a home-and-home with a Pac-12 or Big 12 opponent, sure. But what does it say about belief in your roster?

This is a program that should be scheduling like it expects to compete for championships, not like it's protecting bowl eligibility in Week One.

Tickets for this game are going for $51 on the secondary market. Georgia's FCS opener? $79. Even the ticket market knows the difference between programs scheduling with purpose and programs scheduling with caution.

Tennessee will beat Furman by 40. Fans will leave happy. And come December, we'll be arguing about whether the Vols deserve a playoff spot when their non-conference slate includes an FCS team nobody outside Greenville has heard of.

The season starts September 5th. The real questions start right now.
