## The Pattern Nobody Wants to Name

The Titans have drafted some good players. They've drafted some bad ones. That's not the story.

The story is *how* they've done it. Safely. Quietly. With one eye always on the exit.

Look back across two decades of draft boards and you see the same fingerprint. Tennessee gravitates toward the known commodity. The SEC product. The high-floor, low-ceiling prospect. The guy who won't embarrass anyone in year one.

That's not scouting. That's insurance.

## The Hits That Prove the Rule

Kevin Byard fell to the fifth round in 2016. Derrick Henry dropped to the second in the same class. Both became franchise cornerstones.

Notice anything? Neither was a reach. Neither required conviction. The board handed them to Tennessee.

The front office deserves credit for not screwing those up. But taking a running back who won the Heisman and a safety who started 47 games in college isn't visionary drafting. It's competent shopping.

The real test is who you take when the board goes blank.

## The Misses That Reveal Everything

Jake Locker. First round, 2011. Tennessee spent a top-ten pick on a quarterback they weren't sure about and then never fully committed to.

That's the tell. They drafted him because they needed a quarterback, not because they believed in *him*. The moment doubt crept in, he was done.

Same story with Marcus Mariota. Tremendous talent. Front office that didn't know what to build around him. By the time they figured it out, they'd already moved on.

The Titans don't fail on players. They fail on commitment.

## What It Actually Reveals

This front office — through multiple regimes, multiple GMs — has consistently prioritized avoiding catastrophic failure over chasing greatness.

That's a philosophical choice. And it has a cost.

You don't build a dynasty by drafting to your floor. You build it by betting on upside when the film says bet.

Randy Moss was available in 1998. Tennessee took Kevin Dyson. I'm not saying that was wrong. I'm saying it was characteristic.

## The Question Going Forward

The Will Levis pick in 2023 felt different. It felt like conviction. Like someone in that building actually said *this is our guy* and meant it.

Maybe they've changed. Maybe the culture has shifted.

Or maybe the next hard moment — a slow start, a bad stretch — reveals the same old reflex.

Patterns don't break easy. Especially ones built from fear.
