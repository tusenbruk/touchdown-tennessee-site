## The News, Straight Up

Chaz Coleman is out. Not transferred, not suspended — medically disqualified, which means this one is permanent. The Tennessee edge rusher won't suit up for the Vols again, and that distinction matters more than people are treating it right now.

Medical disqualifications don't get reversed. There's no portal entry, no second act at a G5 school next season. Whatever the condition is — and the program isn't obligated to say — the doctors looked at the situation and pulled the plug on his career. That's a hard outcome for a young man, and it deserves to be acknowledged before we get into the football.

## What It Means for the Defense

Now the football. Tennessee opens Week 1 against Furman at Neyland Stadium on September 5th, and Josh Heupel's staff is already recalculating the edge rotation.

Coleman wasn't a household name — he wasn't getting Mel Kiper breakdowns — but depth at defensive end in the SEC is not a luxury item. It's a requirement. Offensive lines in this conference are built to wear down pass rushers across 60-plus snaps. You need four, five legitimate bodies who can win one-on-one in space.

Losing Coleman compresses that rotation. Whoever was penciled in as the fourth or fifth option at the position just moved up a slot whether they're ready or not.

## The Bigger Picture on the Roster

This news drops in late June, which actually matters. Tennessee still has time to address the void — the transfer portal has a summer window, and Heupel's staff has shown they're not shy about using it. The question is whether they identify a target before fall camp opens or roll with what they have.

Fall camp competition will be real. A developmental player who wasn't expected to contribute in 2026 might suddenly find himself in the two-deep by Week 2. That's how these things shake out.

Furman is a manageable opener. Murray State visits MTSU the same day and nobody's losing sleep over that one either. But the schedule doesn't stay soft. When Tennessee gets deep into SEC play, a thin edge rotation gets exposed — not in September, but in October and November when it counts.

## The Next Question

Heupel addressed this internally before it went public, which means a plan exists. The real question is whether the plan is good enough.

A healthy Chaz Coleman would have answered that question on the field. Now somebody else has to.
