The Titans will open the 2026 season at home against the Jets on September 13th.

Vegas has Tennessee favored by three points. That's a home opener against a team nobody fears.

The over-under sits at 39.5. Read that number again. That's the lowest total you'll see on any NFL slate unless someone's starting their third-string quarterback in a monsoon.

## What The Line Says

The oddsmakers think this will be ugly football. Low-scoring. Defensive. Or just bad.

Neither offense inspires confidence at this distance. The Jets have been rebuilding since the last time they mattered. The Titans haven't exactly lit up scoreboards lately either.

A 39.5 total means Vegas expects something like 21-17. Maybe 24-20 if somebody gets frisky. That's not a compliment to either sideline.

## September In Nashville

Home openers at Nissan Stadium are supposed to mean something. The building gets loud when it wants to. The Titans have historically taken care of business in Week 1.

But three points is barely a line. It says the books respect Tennessee enough to make them favorites, but not enough to make anyone sweat it.

The moneyline has the Titans at -170. That means you'd need to risk $170 to win $100. Those aren't odds that scream dominance. Those are odds that say "probably, but who knows."

## What We Don't Know

It's May. Nobody knows who's starting at quarterback for either team. Nobody knows if the draft picks from this spring will matter by September.

Tickets are going for $61. That's what you pay when the league schedule comes out and nobody's sure what they're buying yet.

But the number is out there now. Titans by three. CBS has the game. 1:00 PM kick. 

Four months from now, we'll know if that 39.5 was a gift or a warning.
