## The Matchup Nobody's Losing Sleep Over

Furman walks into Neyland Stadium on September 5th and, on paper, this is a gimme. The Paladins are a Southern Conference FCS program. Tennessee is a power SEC program with a roster built to compete for CFP spots. The scoreboard outcome is not the story here.

The story is what Tennessee does *with* the game.

## Why Cupcakes Still Matter

Week 1 FCS opponents get a bad rap as meaningless. They're not. They're a controlled environment — the closest thing to a live practice you'll get with stakes attached.

Think of it this way: you want to see your offensive line move defenders off the ball in a phone booth. You want your new starters at linebacker — if there are any — to play fast and instinctive without thinking. You want your quarterback operating on rhythm, not survival mode.

If Tennessee comes out flat, runs the ball sideways, and settles for field goals in the red zone against an FCS defense, that's information. Bad information, but information.

## The Real Questions This Game Answers

First: quarterback command. Who's running this offense and are they running it with authority? A game like Furman should produce clean rhythm throws, quick decisions, and zero turnover-worthy plays. If the signal-caller is hesitant against this competition, that hesitancy doesn't disappear when Georgia or Alabama shows up.

Second: defensive depth. Coordinators will rotate heavily in blowout situations. This is where you find out if your second and third-string guys can hold the fort — or if you're one injury away from a depth crisis later in October.

Third: special teams execution. Kicking games lose close SEC games. Neyland in Week 1 against a lesser opponent is the perfect moment to build confidence and consistency in that unit.

## Keep One Eye on Athens

While Tennessee handles business at home, Georgia opens the same weekend against Tennessee State — a different Tigers squad wearing blue and gold from Nashville. That's a rivalry-adjacent data point worth tracking. The Bulldogs are always the measuring stick in the SEC East.

How UGA looks in Week 1 recalibrates expectations for the whole conference.

## Bottom Line

Tennessee should win this game by multiple scores. The margin isn't the question — the *method* is. Clean football, fast starts, and healthy bodies coming out of Week 1 set the table for everything that follows.

The 2026 season doesn't get decided on September 5th. But it can absolutely get complicated.
