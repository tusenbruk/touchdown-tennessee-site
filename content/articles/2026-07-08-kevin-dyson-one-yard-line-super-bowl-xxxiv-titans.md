## The Tackle Everyone Remembers Wrong

Mike Jones made the play. Fine. Give him his credit.

But here's what nobody wants to sit with: Kevin Dyson didn't fail. The route was right. The catch was clean. He got every inch his body allowed him to get. That ball was spotted at the one. He fell forward. Jones held on.

The difference between dynasty and tragedy was roughly the length of your forearm.

## What That Play Actually Demanded

People talk about that final second like Dyson was supposed to teleport. He caught the ball at the five. He had one man to beat and no time to beat him. What the play *required* — what it would have taken to score — was for a professional linebacker to simply miss.

Jones didn't miss.

That's the part that should haunt you. The Titans didn't lose because of a bad call. They didn't lose because of a fumble or a turnover or a coaching blunder. They lost because the other team made an extraordinary play in an extraordinary moment.

That's a harder kind of loss to carry.

## Steve McNair Kept Them Alive

Forget Jones and Dyson for a second. Rewind ninety seconds.

Steve McNair — on a bad knee, against the best defense in football — drove seventy-three yards with no timeouts. He scrambled for twelve yards on a busted play. He took hits and kept moving. He put this team in position to win a Super Bowl on the final play.

McNair didn't lose that game. He *almost won it.*

That distinction matters. The 1999 Titans were not a team that stumbled into a Super Bowl. They were a legitimate championship roster that ran out of inches.

## The Weight of Almost

Tennessee hasn't been back to the Super Bowl since. That's twenty-five years of *almost* sitting in the building like old furniture you never throw out.

You wonder sometimes what a championship in 2000 does for this franchise. Does it change the free agent calculus? Does it keep McNair here longer? Does it make this city a different kind of football town?

We'll never know. One yard erased the answer.

## The Real Question Nobody Asks

Here's what I keep coming back to: If Jones misses that tackle — if Dyson scores — does Tennessee win the decade?

One yard. One arm. One second.

The Titans have been chasing that yard ever since.
