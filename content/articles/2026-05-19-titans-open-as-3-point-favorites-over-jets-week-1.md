The Titans will open the 2026 season at Nissan Stadium against the Jets on September 13th.

Vegas has them favored by three points.

That's a home favorite line. Nothing more, nothing less. The kind of spread you get when the house thinks you're marginally better than a bad team.

## The Total Is the Story

The over/under sits at 39.5 points. That's the lowest Week 1 total I've seen in years.

DraftKings isn't projecting a shootout. They're projecting a slog. Two teams that can't move the ball consistently, settling for field goals and three-and-outs.

A 39.5 total suggests final scores like 20-17 or 23-16. Football from a different era. The kind of game that makes you check your phone in the third quarter.

## What It Means

The front office will spin this as respect. Home favorite in Week 1. CBS national broadcast. Tickets starting at $61.

But the truth lives in that total.

Vegas doesn't trust either offense. They're not projecting quarterback play that scares anybody. They're betting on punts and incompletions.

The Jets haven't been relevant in years. They're the kind of opponent you schedule when you want a soft landing in Week 1. And we're only favored by a field goal.

## September 13th

That's 116 days from now. Training camp will answer some questions. The draft will answer others.

But right now, in mid-May, the betting markets think this team is marginally better than the Jets and incapable of scoring 24 points at home.

That's where we are.

The season opener should feel like a statement game. Instead, it feels like two teams Vegas expects to finish third in their divisions, playing in front of 7,000 empty seats.

The spread will move between now and September. It always does.

But that 39.5 total? That number doesn't lie.
