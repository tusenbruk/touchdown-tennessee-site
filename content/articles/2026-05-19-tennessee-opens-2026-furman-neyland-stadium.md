Tennessee will host Furman on September 5, 2026 to open the season. Time is not yet set.

Furman runs the option. They always run the option. Clay Hendrix has coached there since 2013 and the Paladins haven't stopped running it once in that stretch. Tennessee faced them in 1973 and won 42-0. That was the last time.

The SoCon program went 12-2 in 2021 and reached the FCS quarterfinals. They still run the option.

## The Georgia Wrinkle

Georgia scheduled Tennessee State for the same day. Sanford Stadium. Also time TBD. Tickets there start at $79, which tells you something about how the programs value these games differently.

Tennessee State plays in Nashville. They won the Big South–OVC championship in 2023. Eddie George coaches them now, which gives the program name recognition but doesn't change the fundamental math of an HBCU program playing a top-10 SEC team in Athens.

Both Tennessee and Georgia need September wins before conference play. Furman brings 63 scholarships instead of 85. Tennessee State brings fewer resources than that.

## What the Opener Means

Neyland will have over 4,000 tickets available at $51 as of now. The secondary market says this game won't sell out, which tracks for an early September FCS matchup. Tennessee hasn't struggled to beat FCS competition under Josh Heupel. UT-Martin scored three points in 2022. Austin Peay scored six in 2021.

Furman will take more time off the clock. The triple option does that. Expect long drives that stall at the 40. Expect Tennessee to score quickly when it has the ball. Expect the second half to feature walk-ons.

The interesting part comes after. Tennessee's full 2026 schedule isn't public yet. Neither is the SEC slate. This opener buys the Vols a week to work out depth chart questions before someone real shows up.

Furman hasn't beaten an FBS team since 2019 when they took down Charlotte. Tennessee last lost to an FCS opponent in 1982 against Duke, back when Duke played 1-AA ball for one season.

The option will test gap discipline. That's about it.
